Where did it come from?

Typography goes back as far as humans began printing. It dates back to as early as the second millenium B.C. where humans would use it to make seals and currency in ancient times. Modern Typography came around during the Digital Age, where it became digitized in Computers to display messages on computer screens and implemented in programming.



Cyrillic

Modern writing System used in Eastern European Countries and North Asia. It was named after Cyril and Methodius, and adopted for local Slavic Languages when Christianity was spreading.

  • Script


Hieroglyphics

Why do we need it?

In UX Design, Typography has become a very important pillar visually. It helps to make a webpage or product more friendly and relatable to the user. It can unconsciously sell a message or point through ways of aesthetic pleasure. In terms of Psychology and the two-factor theory, It's the cherry on top of a great User Experience and therefore a great motivator. That's what makes it so important to Design and people.

The Future



Disclaimer: Due to Typography being a part of Visual Design, it can be subject to fashion trends and fads. One typeface that is popular today may be "last year" tomorrow.

Typography is an ever changing field. It employs hundreds of designers in creating fonts every year, but one problem with this is the strength of powerhouses. The fonts that have been around for ages and don't need to leave. This can make it difficult for typeface designers to sell to the market. Therefore they are becoming more alternative in their approaches such as extreme sizing, superimposed fonts on images, and more artistic fonts in general. These are the trends that you can expect to keep Typography going.

Overall

It makes things easier for the world in the sense of being able to read content on web pages and involve aesthetic into an ever changing online world.
